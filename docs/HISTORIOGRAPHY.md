# Historiography — grounding the game in the new history of alchemy

Every tile, upgrade, and disaster is anchored in the *new historiography of alchemy*:
the scholarship, from roughly the 1990s on, that recovered early modern **chymistry** as
a rigorous, reproducible, quantitative craft — dissolving the old "good chemistry vs.
bad alchemy" split and taking chrysopoeia (gold-making) seriously as an experimental
pursuit. The educational mission (from Ted's founding pitch) is to teach exactly this.
Design rule: flavor and mechanics may only assert what this scholarship supports.

## The scholars and their load-bearing arguments

**William R. Newman & Lawrence M. Principe.**
- "Alchemy vs. Chemistry: The Etymological Origins of a Historiographic Mistake,"
  *Early Science and Medicine* 3 (1998): 32–65 — the case for the neutral term
  **chymistry** and against reading modern "chemistry" back onto the period.
- *Alchemy Tried in the Fire: Starkey, Boyle, and the Fate of Helmontian Chymistry*
  (Chicago, 2002) — George Starkey's **laboratory notebooks** show planned, quantitative,
  reproducible experiment; Boyle learned chymistry from this milieu, not against it.
- Newman, *Gehennical Fire: The Lives of George Starkey* (Harvard, 1994).
- Newman, *Promethean Ambitions: Alchemy and the Quest to Perfect Nature* (Chicago, 2004)
  — the art-versus-nature debate; alchemists as would-be perfecters of nature.
- Newman, *Atoms and Alchemy* (Chicago, 2006) — chymistry and the experimental origins of
  matter theory.

**Lawrence M. Principe.**
- *The Secrets of Alchemy* (Chicago, 2013) — decoding **Decknamen** (cover-names) and the
  laboratory **reconstruction** of historical processes, including the **Philosophers'
  Tree** (*arbor Dianae*), a real dendritic silver growth alchemists read as a sign of
  metallic life.

**Bruce T. Moran.**
- *Distilling Knowledge: Alchemy, Chemistry, and the Scientific Revolution* (Harvard, 2005)
  — **distillation** and the arts of fire as the craft matrix from which the Scientific
  Revolution partly grew; knowledge made with the hands.
- *Andreas Libavius and the Transformation of Alchemy* (2007); *The Alchemical World of
  the German Court* (1991) — **court patronage** and the laboratory as a real workspace.

**Supporting material-culture scholarship.**
- Marcos Martinón-Torres & Thilo Rehren on **Hessian crucibles** (archaeometry: mullite,
  refractoriness) — the material-culture turn the above historians endorse.
- Pamela H. Smith, *The Body of the Artisan* (2004) — **artisanal epistemology**,
  knowledge in the hands and senses.
- Primary loci cited in flavor: Biringuccio, *De la pirotechnia* (1540); Agricola,
  *De re metallica* (1556); John of Rupescissa on the quintessence (14th c.).

## Mapping: game element → apparatus/practice → scholarship

The board is **pure laboratory apparatus** — no Reading Desk, no Patron's Cabinet. Every
tile is a real operation of the working lab, cross-referenced to the 100 facts in
ALCHEMY_FACTS.md.

| Element | Historical anchor | Scholarship |
|---|---|---|
| **Mortar & Pestle** (grind/prepare → ingredients) | levigation of minerals, the skilled first operation | Pamela Smith on artisanal epistemology; Newman & Principe |
| **Alembic** (distil → metal/gold) | alembic & cucurbit; distillation as the signature operation | Moran, *Distilling Knowledge* (2005) |
| **Crucible** (fire → potions/medicine) | Hessian crucibles, mullite, high-temp metallurgy | Martinón-Torres & Rehren; material-culture turn |
| **Assay Balance** (weigh & prove → technique upgrade) | the balance made chymistry quantitative; weigh before/after | Newman & Principe, *Alchemy Tried in the Fire* (2002) |
| **Furnace Hood** (negate first sickening) | flue/hood over the furnace; Uraniborg mercury/arsenic/lead residues | Moran; Uraniborg excavation (1988–90) |
| **Cupellation Furnace** (assay & refine → VP) | cupellation, parting, cementation — the fire assay proves fine metal | assay archaeology; Roos, *The Salt of the Earth* |
| **Quenching Trough** (free heal) | water against burns/fire in the furnace-house | Biringuccio (1540); Agricola (1556); Moran |
| **Salt-of-Tartar Bath** (cancel an acid disaster) | fixed alkali (potash) neutralizes the mineral acids | Roos, *The Salt of the Earth*; Newman & Principe |
| **Cohobation** (research upgrade: alembic +1) | redistilling the draw-off to enrich yield | Moran, *Distilling Knowledge* |
| **The Chrysopoeia** (endgame gamble) | the **Philosophers' Tree** / *arbor Dianae*, reconstructed in the lab | Principe, *The Secrets of Alchemy* (2013) |
| **Disaster deck** (aqua fortis, mercury, antimony, vitriol) | genuine hazards of chymical work | the danger is the counterpoint to chymistry's real efficacy |

## What we deliberately do NOT do

- No legendary or fictional alchemists (no Flamel/Perenelle); the roster is the
  AlchemyTimelineMap scholarly database only.
- No invented "medieval quotations" (the 2024 brainstorm's flavor is retired).
- No portmanteau apparatus ("elixir pot," "philosopher's table"); only attested names.
- Chrysopoeia is framed as a serious experimental pursuit that *sometimes visibly
  succeeds* (the silver tree), never as mere fraud — per Newman & Principe.
